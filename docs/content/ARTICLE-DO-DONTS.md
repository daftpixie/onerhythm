# Article Do / Don'ts

## Tone Do's

- Lead with truth and land with humanity.
- Keep the writing warm, serious, and emotionally literate.
- Be explicit about invisible burden without turning suffering into spectacle.
- Let gratitude, evidence, and structural clarity coexist when writing to institutions.
- Write as if the reader is intelligent, busy, and carrying something real.

## Tone Don'ts

- Do not sound accusatory when writing to clinicians or institutions.
- Do not sound like a startup blog, press release, or SEO content farm.
- Do not use inflated medical certainty.
- Do not flatten lived experience into sterile clinical abstraction.
- Do not perform pain for emotional leverage.

## Logo And Wordmark Rules

- Use canonical assets from `apps/web/public/brand/logos/`.
- Use `onerhythm-full-white.svg` or `onerhythm-full-gradient-800.png` on dark surfaces.
- Do not recreate the wordmark in text.
- Do not recolor the mark or stretch the lockup.
- Do not place the wordmark on busy backgrounds without controlled contrast.

## Typography Rules

- Exo 2: headlines and display only.
- DM Sans: body copy, utility text, UI copy.
- Space Mono: labels, metadata, document utility text, and compact evidence markers.
- Never use Exo 2 below 18px.
- Keep paragraph width comfortable and line height generous enough for serious reading.

## Pull Quote Rules

- Use pull quotes to clarify the argument, not to decorate the page.
- Pull quotes should be short enough to scan and strong enough to stand alone.
- Prefer lines that reveal the article's thesis or emotional center.
- Do not stack too many pull quotes on one page.
- Do not use pull quotes that overstate certainty or imply diagnosis.

## Statistic And Citation Rules

- Only use statistics that are already supported by reviewed sources or approved editorial strategy.
- If exact source metadata is not verified, do not invent DOI, PMID, or publisher details.
- Present distress statistics with context and restraint.
- Pair burden statistics with a structural implication, not just emotional reaction.
- Keep full source cards on the article page rather than cluttering body paragraphs with heavy academic footnotes.

## Social Excerpt Rules

- Share copy should stay dignified, useful, and human.
- X copy should be concise and sharp without becoming alarmist.
- LinkedIn copy should sound authoritative and institution-ready.
- Reddit copy should work in short paragraphs and TL;DR form.
- Image-card snippets should be short enough to sit on branded visuals cleanly.
- Do not write social copy that implies OneRhythm offers clinical analysis.

## Forbidden Diagnostic Language

- "we detected"
- "your ECG shows"
- "this proves you have"
- "your rhythm means"
- "you likely have"
- "we can tell from your waveform"
- "this predicts your outcome"
- "this article should replace your doctor"

## Publishing QA Checklist

- Is the route role clear: homepage teaser vs dedicated reading page?
- Does the page preserve the dark-first OneRhythm brand system?
- Is the disclaimer present where required?
- Are crisis resources present if suicidality or self-harm is mentioned?
- Are all claims backed by structured sources or approved editorial framing?
- Does the Open Letter remain respectful and non-accusatory?
- Does Living Inside the Numbers stay evidence-grounded and broader than one patient story?
- Does the OG title/description/image match the article's real tone?
- Does the share kit avoid clinical overclaiming?
- Does the print/export mode still read as OneRhythm and remain legible?
