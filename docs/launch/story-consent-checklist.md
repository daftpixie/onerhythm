# Story Consent Checklist

Use this checklist before publishing any personal narrative or community story.

## Consent gate

- The story has explicit public-story-sharing consent on record.
- Consent is still granted and not revoked.
- The consent record matches the story owner and the submitted story.
- The story has passed the moderation workflow.

Launch blocker if:

- explicit consent is missing
- consent is revoked
- the story has not completed review

## Privacy review

- Display mode matches the approved setting:
  - first name / display name
  - pseudonym
- No email, phone number, address, provider name, facility name, or other identifying detail appears in the public copy unless intentionally approved and necessary.
- Third-party details are minimized.
- The story does not expose private upload, profile, or account data.

Launch blocker if:

- the story reveals identifying information beyond the approved display rule
- the story leaks another person’s private information

## Safety and scope review

- The story remains a personal narrative, not a medical advice surface.
- Clinical claims are not added or strengthened by editing.
- Any mention of diagnosis or treatment remains personal context, not a general recommendation.
- The story does not instruct readers to start, stop, or avoid specific treatment.

Launch blocker if:

- the story reads as advice or instruction
- editing has introduced clinical authority that the story does not have

## Tone review

- Emotional truth remains intact.
- Editing did not flatten the voice into institutional language.
- The page does not turn distress into spectacle.
- The narrative fits the OneRhythm voice: humane, steady, and respectful.

## Publication check

- Story status is `published`.
- Slug, title, summary, and author display are correct.
- The story page renders without exposing draft or moderation metadata.
