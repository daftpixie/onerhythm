# Public Copy Review Checklist

Use this checklist for launch review of homepage, mission pages, advocacy pages, evidence pages, and other public entry surfaces.

## Core narrative checks

- The page reflects the campaign strategy:
  - lead with reality
  - land with humanity
- The public mission is understandable in one pass.
- The page explains what OneRhythm is and what it is not.
- The non-diagnostic boundary is preserved where relevant.

## Distinction checks

- Founder-origin copy is clearly narrative and mission-driven.
- Research and evidence copy is clearly evidence-driven.
- Advocacy language is not confused with educational guidance.
- The Heart Mosaic is described as collective proof of solidarity, not a clinical display.

Launch blocker if:

- narrative and evidence are blended in a misleading way
- the page makes unsupported medical-adjacent claims

## Tone checks

- The page sounds warm, precise, and emotionally literate.
- The page avoids alarmist framing.
- The page avoids startup hype, inflated promises, and sensational phrasing.
- The page respects people with lived experience rather than speaking about them at a distance.

## Brand and design checks

- Copy length and hierarchy fit the current design system.
- Headings, subheads, and CTA labels feel consistent with the brand voice.
- Key entry points are clear:
  - contribute
  - learn
  - explore
- Public pages feel cohesive with the dark-first brand language.

## Accessibility and clarity checks

- CTA labels are specific and understandable out of context.
- Link text is not vague.
- Reading level is plain-language enough for a public audience.
- Dense evidence or policy sections are broken into readable chunks.

## Launch blockers

Do not ship if:

- a page sounds diagnostic or clinical
- a page contains unsupported claims
- a page hides the platform boundary
- a page misuses founder narrative as evidence
- a page undermines trust with hype or ambiguity
