# Campaign And Content Strategy

This document is the implementation-facing markdown companion to the canonical campaign source PDF at [docs/brand/OneRhythm_Campaign_Content_Strategy.pdf](../OneRhythm_Campaign_Content_Strategy.pdf). It exists to help contributors reference the strategy in plain text while the PDF remains the primary source of truth for content and layout decisions.

## Why this exists

OneRhythm exists because rhythm disorders often create a second burden beyond the physical condition itself: fear, isolation, disrupted identity, and an ongoing mental health toll that is easy for the outside world to miss.

The platform is being built to make that experience more visible, less isolating, and easier to navigate through:

- a public heart mosaic built from de-identified ECG contributions
- calm, profile-based educational guidance
- privacy-preserving product boundaries
- transparent open-source implementation

## Project origin

OneRhythm is grounded in the lived experience of its builder, Matthew Adams, and in the broader reality that arrhythmia care often leaves psychological and emotional burden under-addressed.

That origin matters for the product:

- the platform should speak with humanity, not institutional distance
- community presence is a core feature, not marketing decoration
- educational content should help people feel less alone without crossing into diagnosis or treatment

## Strategy pillars

### Visibility

Make the invisible weight of arrhythmias legible to the public through careful storytelling, evidence-backed framing, and respectful design.

In practice:

- explain the emotional and mental health burden clearly
- avoid sensational or fear-based framing
- pair lived experience with credible source material

### Community

Use the Heart Mosaic as a communal symbol of shared presence rather than a clinical display.

In practice:

- the mosaic should feel artistic, luminous, and collective
- public tile metadata must stay anonymous
- contribution flows must center consent and privacy

### Education

Provide condition-specific educational support that helps people prepare, ask better questions, and find credible resources.

In practice:

- education must be based on self-reported profile data plus approved sources
- educational content must remain non-diagnostic
- support resources and mental health context belong in the product, not as an afterthought

## Voice and tone

OneRhythm should sound like a knowledgeable companion.

Required qualities:

- precise
- warm
- emotionally literate
- evidence-aware
- direct without being cold

Rules:

- lead with reality, then offer steadiness
- never write as if the product is diagnosing or treating someone
- avoid hype, miracle framing, or startup self-congratulation
- do not turn pain into spectacle
- speak with conviction when naming the problem and the platform boundary

## What belongs in product

- a homepage that clearly communicates purpose, privacy, and non-diagnostic boundaries
- an about/origin surface that explains why OneRhythm exists
- a Heart Mosaic that feels like the emotional centerpiece of the platform
- educational pages that include persistent disclaimers and visible source provenance
- support-resource pathways that acknowledge emotional burden respectfully

## What belongs in docs and operations

- editorial review workflow for approved sources
- monthly curated-content refresh process
- public messaging guidance for contributors and maintainers
- launch and release checklists for public communications

## Launch content priorities

Before public launch, the repository and product should be able to support:

- a clear founder-origin narrative
- a concise explanation of the mental health and community problem OneRhythm addresses
- a strong public framing of the Heart Mosaic
- educational guidance with visible provenance and persistent disclaimers
- open-source transparency about privacy, consent, export, and deletion behavior

## Measurement

The strategy favors meaningful trust and engagement signals over vanity metrics.

Examples:

- whether people understand the non-diagnostic boundary
- whether contributors understand consent and privacy choices
- whether educational pages are being used and reviewed
- whether people report feeling more informed and less alone

## Product boundaries

This strategy does not change the platform safety model.

OneRhythm must remain:

- educational, not diagnostic
- profile-driven for educational outputs
- separate between self-reported profile data and anonymized mosaic data
- privacy-preserving in upload handling
- optional about blockchain and similar provenance systems
