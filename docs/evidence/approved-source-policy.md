# Approved Source Policy

This document describes the launch policy for the curated educational corpus in `apps/api/app/content/approved_sources.json`.

It is intentionally narrower than the broader public-content source policy.

## Scope

This policy applies to profile-driven educational guidance generated by the API.

It does not govern:

- general static editorial pages
- founder-story or campaign narrative pages
- Research Pulse publication review

The educational boundary remains fixed:

- educational output must stay non-diagnostic
- educational output must remain based on self-reported profile fields plus approved external sources
- ECG uploads and ECG-derived artifacts are out of scope for this corpus

## Source hierarchy implemented now

The launch corpus uses a strict source hierarchy.

Required grounding by section:

- `condition_education` must be grounded in a `clinical_guideline`
- `doctor_questions` must be grounded in a `clinical_guideline`
- `recent_advancements` must be grounded in a `peer_reviewed_study`
- `mental_health_resources` must be grounded in a `peer_reviewed_study`

Additional rules:

- the curated corpus must use an external `publisher_kind`
- allowed external publisher kinds are `journal`, `professional_society`, `health_system`, and `institution`
- `onerhythm_editorial`, `founder`, and `internal` are not allowed in the launch educational corpus
- `institutional_reference` is supported by the data model, but it is not a substitute for the required guideline and peer-reviewed coverage above

## Required metadata and review expectations

Every approved-source entry must currently include:

- `locale`
- `diagnosis_code`
- `content_section`
- `evidence_kind`
- `title`
- `summary`
- `source_name`
- `source_url`
- `reviewed_at`
- `reviewer_ref`
- structured `provenance`

Current provenance requirements:

- `registry_source_id`
- `source_document_id`
- `ingested_from`
- `source_classification`
- `publisher_kind`
- `monthly_update_cycle`
- `approval_status = approved`
- `citation.citation_label`
- diagnosis-aligned `topic_tags`
- `usage_scope` including both `educational_guidance` and the matching content section

Current citation expectations:

- guidelines must record `guideline_body` plus a `DOI` or `PMID`
- peer-reviewed studies must record a `DOI` or `PMID`
- if a field is not verified, it should be left blank rather than invented

## Implemented repository behavior

The repository already enforces the policy above in code.

Implemented now:

- approved-source files are validated before ingestion
- the launch corpus must cover every supported launch diagnosis with all required sections
- required topic coverage is validated for each launch diagnosis
- educational generation fails closed when curated content is missing, incomplete, stale, or grounded in non-external source material
- educational responses expose source metadata back to the caller for provenance

## Review window and freshness

Freshness is currently enforced through reviewed metadata and a staleness gate.

Current behavior:

- the corpus records `reviewed_at` and `monthly_update_cycle`
- the API computes corpus staleness from reviewed timestamps
- stale curated content is blocked rather than silently served

Important limitation:

- `monthly_update_cycle` is metadata, not an automated review scheduler
- the repository does not yet include a dedicated editor UI or reminder system for corpus refresh

## What remains future work

The following areas are not fully built yet:

- editor-facing approval and review tooling
- automated source-refresh scheduling
- multi-locale launch corpus management
- durable storage of past educational responses for historical auditing
